### Data Source Reliability

#### Primary Data Sources
**Data Source 1: Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Current Population Survey**
- **Reliability Rating:** Very High
- **Data Collection Method:** Monthly household survey of ~60,000 households selected through probability sampling
- **Update Frequency:** Monthly data collection; annual averages published annually
- **Historical Availability:** Consistent data back to 1970s; some series back to 1940s
- **Known Limitations:** Self-reported employment status; sampling error Â±0.2-0.5 percentage points for major demographics; doesn't capture underground economy

**Data Source 2: Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) EEO-1 Data**
- **Reliability Rating:** High (with caveats)
- **Data Collection Method:** Employer self-reporting annually; required for employers with 100+ employees
- **Update Frequency:** Annual collection; aggregate public data released with 3-5 year lag
- **Historical Availability:** Data collected since 1966; public aggregate data varies by release
- **Known Limitations:** Not all required employers file; self-reported by employers; smaller employers exempt; significant publication lag

**Data Source 3: USAFacts**
- **Reliability Rating:** High (derivative source)
- **Data Collection Method:** Aggregates and visualizes federal government data (primarily from BLS, Census Bureau)
- **Update Frequency:** Updated as source data (BLS, Census) is published
- **Historical Availability:** Depends on underlying federal data sources
- **Known Limitations:** Derivative source (not original data); relies on federal data quality; visualization choices may emphasize certain patterns

#### Data Integration Assessment
**Cross-System Consistency:** High - BLS and EEOC data show consistent patterns; USAFacts derivative from BLS
**Data Definition Consistency:** High - Federal sources use standardized OMB race/ethnicity definitions
**Temporal Alignment:** Good - Most data from same time periods (2013-2024); EEOC lags by 3-5 years

### Bias Assessment

#### Selection Bias
**Data Availability Bias:** 
- **Risk Level:** Low
- **Issue:** BLS CPS uses probability sampling; EEOC requires reporting from large employers (100+ employees)
- **Impact:** Small employers (<100 employees) not included in EEOC data; BLS sample-based so rare populations have wider error margins

**Survivorship Bias:**
- **Risk Level:** Low
- **Issue:** BLS captures all employed and unemployed; EEOC captures active employers
- **Impact:** Minimal - data includes both employed and unemployed workers

#### Measurement Bias
**Gaming/Manipulation Risk:**
- **Risk Level:** Low (BLS), Moderate (EEOC)
- **Issue:** BLS is independent household survey, hard to manipulate; EEOC is employer self-reported
- **Mitigation:** BLS uses trained interviewers and standardized protocols; EEOC data is legally required filings with penalties for false reporting

**Reporting Bias:**
- **Risk Level:** Low
